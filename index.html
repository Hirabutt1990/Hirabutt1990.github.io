My work integrates pluripotent stem cell biology, CRISPR/Cas9 genome editing, mesenchymal stem cell therapies, disease modeling, nanomedicine, and cGMP-compliant cell manufacturing — with applications spanning tissue regeneration, inherited disease correction, wound healing, and translational cell therapy. I have a strong track record of first-author publications in high-impact journals, competitive fellowship awards, and mentorship of junior scientists.

Research Interests

01

Pluripotent Stem Cell Biology & Directed Differentiation

I work with human embryonic stem cells (hESCs) and induced pluripotent stem cells (iPSCs) to generate clinically relevant cell types for regenerative therapies. My work spans naïve and primed pluripotency, directed differentiation protocols, transcriptomic validation, and functional benchmarking of derived cell types — applicable across diverse tissue and disease contexts.

hESC / iPSCNaïve PluripotencyDirected DifferentiationTranscriptomicsCell Characterization
02

CRISPR/Cas9 Genome Editing & Disease Correction

I design and execute CRISPR/Cas9 strategies to model and correct monogenic diseases, including gene knockout and precise knock-in approaches. My experience spans sgRNA design, electroporation, Sanger sequencing validation, and flow cytometric phenotyping of edited cell lines — with a focus on therapeutic translation across multiple inherited disease contexts.

CRISPR/Cas9 KO & KIsgRNA DesignElectroporationSanger SequencingFlow Cytometry
03

Mesenchymal Stem Cells & Tissue Regeneration

I have extensive experience isolating, culturing, and characterizing MSCs from umbilical cord, adipose tissue, and bone marrow. My doctoral research focused on antioxidant preconditioning strategies (Vitamin E, EGCG, curcumin) to enhance MSC cytoprotection and therapeutic efficacy in wound healing, burn injury, and skin regeneration — validated in rodent in vivo models.

Umbilical Cord MSCsAdipose MSCsCell PreconditioningWound HealingBurn ModelsDiabetic Wound
04

Biomaterials, Nanomedicine & Drug Delivery

I develop and characterize biomaterial platforms and nanoparticle systems for cell delivery and controlled drug release. This includes hydrogel fabrication, amniotic membrane scaffold preparation, and synthesis of nanoparticles (zinc oxide, magnetic, oxygen-generating nanofibers) for tissue engineering and targeted therapeutic delivery.

Nanoparticle SynthesisHydrogel FabricationDrug DeliveryTissue EngineeringAngiogenesis
05

Clinical Translation & cGMP Cell Manufacturing

I have led cGMP-compliant stem cell laboratory operations supporting clinical-grade cell therapy development, including a registered Phase I/II clinical trial. My translational expertise spans IRB protocol development, quality control testing, regulatory audit management, and bridging basic discoveries toward clinical application in cell therapy.

cGMP ManufacturingClinical TrialsIRB / RegulatoryQuality ControlCell Therapy
