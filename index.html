Who I Am

About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Fellow at the Wilmer Eye Institute, Johns Hopkins University, where I investigate stem cell–based and gene-editing approaches for ocular diseases including glaucoma and lens pathologies. My research bridges the gap between fundamental stem cell biology and clinical translation.

My work spans iPSC and hESC biology, CRISPR/Cas9-mediated genome editing, nanomedicine, biomaterials, and preclinical in vivo disease models. I have conducted research across three internationally recognized institutions on two continents — building a uniquely broad and deep skill set in regenerative medicine.

I am a recipient of the Maryland Stem Cell Research Fund (MSCRF) Postdoctoral Fellowship (2025), the HEC Indigenous Ph.D. Fellowship, and the IRSIP International Research Fellowship (University of Cambridge, UK). My work has led to multiple first-author publications in high-impact journals, a co-authored patent, and international conference presentations.

Professional Experience

🔭
Postdoctoral Fellow 2023 – Present
Wilmer Eye Institute, Johns Hopkins University · Baltimore, Maryland, USA
Project I: Glaucoma — Cell-Based & Gene-Editing Approaches
  • Developed a two-step differentiation protocol for generating hESC-derived trabecular meshwork (TM) cells, benchmarked against primary TM cells and corneal endothelial controls.
  • Transcriptomic profiling and comparative analysis of differentiated TM cells to assess lineage specificity and functional similarity to native TM tissue.
  • Developed in vivo rabbit models (denuded TM and laser-induced TM injury) to evaluate cell integration and therapeutic potential.
  • Conducted magnetic nanoparticle labeling and physicochemical characterization; directed labeled cells to the iridocorneal angle for targeted delivery.
  • Designed and executed CRISPR/Cas9-based experiments for therapeutic intervention in primary congenital glaucoma.
Project II: Lens — Calpain-3 Proteolysis & Autophagosome Transport
  • Proteomic profiling of FYCO1⁻/⁻ mouse lens fiber cells using 2D-GE and 2D-DIGE; analysis of LC3B, MST1, CAPN3 expression.
  • Generated and validated a knock-in LC3(T50A) human epithelial cell line via Sanger sequencing; characterized via flow cytometry.
  • Assessed retrograde autophagosome transport in HLE cells via nucleofection-mediated JIP1/RILP knockdown.
  • Co-authored manuscript on pluripotent stem cell–derived corneal endothelial cells, published in Translational Vision Science & Technology (2025).

What I Investigate

Research Interests

My research focuses on harnessing the regenerative potential of stem cells and precision genome editing to develop transformative therapies for ocular and systemic diseases.

👁️
Glaucoma & Trabecular Meshwork Regeneration
Developing hESC-derived trabecular meshwork (TM) cells for cell replacement therapy in glaucoma. Integrating magnetic nanoparticle–guided targeted delivery and CRISPR/Cas9 correction of primary congenital glaucoma mutations.
hESC Differentiation TM Cells Nanoparticles Rabbit Models
✂️
CRISPR/Cas9 Gene Editing
Engineering precision genome corrections for inherited ocular diseases, including primary congenital glaucoma. Validating knock-in cell lines and investigating CRISPR-based therapeutic strategies in clinically relevant models.
CRISPR/Cas9 Knock-In / Knock-Out sgRNA Design Functional Validation
🔬
iPSC / hESC Biology & Disease Modeling
Generating and characterizing patient-derived iPSCs for disease modeling and drug screening. Investigating pluripotency maintenance, naïve-to-primed transitions, and directed differentiation toward therapeutic cell types.
iPSCs Naïve Pluripotency Disease Modeling Transcriptomics
🩹
Wound Healing & Regenerative Medicine
Developing antioxidant-primed MSC therapies and biomaterial constructs for burn and diabetic wound healing. Investigating AKT/ERK signaling pathways and novel hydrogel/amniotic membrane scaffolds for enhanced skin regeneration.
MSC Preconditioning Hydrogels Diabetic Wound Burn Models
💊
Nanomedicine & Drug Delivery
Synthesizing and characterizing nanoparticles (zinc oxide, magnetic, oxygen-generating nanofibers) for targeted therapeutic delivery. Developing smart hydrogel systems for controlled drug release in regenerative applications.
Nanoparticle Synthesis Drug Delivery Hydrogel Systems Angiogenesis
🧫
Clinical Translation & cGMP Operations
Translating bench discoveries to clinical-grade stem cell therapies. Expertise in cGMP-compliant facility management, IRB protocol development, clinical trial support, and regulatory compliance for cellular therapies.
cGMP Clinical Trials IRB Quality Control
